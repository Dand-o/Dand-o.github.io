Task 1

In terms of an audience for this particular type of research and topic, firstly, it would be important to find literature that provides insight into the information literacy of online users of social media platforms. We will be focusing mostly on Twitter/X.


Twitter was once a (mostly) reliable source of real time news and information, with many ways to verify sources of the information we found on the website. However, there have been recent changes to the platform, many of which make it more difficult to identify the real news or accurate information we consume on the platform.


The main audience we want to target would be casual users of the social media platform, those who seek out breaking news and stories on there. Many of the recent changes have changed the landscape of the platform, making what was once an easily identifiable good source of information, now much easier to spot. The casual user may not be aware of the potential inaccuracy of information, taking what they see from "blue tick" accounts as factual, as previously a blue tick meant an account was verified by Twitter as being a person of importance or relevance within the media or other organisations. Now, anyone can pay for this "verification" and in doing so, we have seen a rise in bot accounts powered by AI algorithms that often tweet current trending topics with incorrect sources or media.


Creating a project that can educate on the warning signs of inaccurate information, challenging their perspectives on what is "real" and what is "fake" news and providing facts about the importance of information literacy in the age of social media, we can hopefully prevent people falling for misinformation and being cautious about the information they consume. By providing an information source, which educates and challenges current views, it will hopefully spread the knowledge of spotting the warning signs and prevent such a widespread problem of people spreading bad information throughout the internet.
