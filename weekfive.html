Task One

As expected, the readability index of this website is quite high. When viewing the website, I did feel a sense that it was easy to digest with bitesize pieces of information and clear and simple language. When we think of Misinformation on Social media, we know that this is an "online" space, and the term "online" is the most frequently used word in this webpage. I do find the webpage to have a good variety of sentences and such, although, when you visualise the data, it does show there is a repetition of words such as "online", "media" and "internet". When consuming the content, I didn't feel like this was an issue, or something that stood out to me, mostly because these words are use in the correct context and are used appropriately. The average word per sentence is also unsurprising to me, 30 words isn't much, and it is very evident when you read the page that you can get your information quickly and without much searching. It's there and it's quick to read.

Here we can see the use of "positive" and "negative" language. In this websites case, much of the negative language is linked to a direct discussion of a topic, and not the use of negative language to describe or tell their narrative. Their advice, and own words are mostly positive such as "encourage", "Support" and "safe". They seem to be aware of the impact of these negative words and provide alternative, positive language.
