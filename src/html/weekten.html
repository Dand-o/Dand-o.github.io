IL Learning

Recording my evaluation and reflecting on own practice.

I chose the BBC Bitesize website to check on these tools, mostly because they have a similar interactive element to the one I would like to implement. Surprisingly some of the tests were a fail on this. I wasn't surprised that the obvious pieces of text were fine, like the black text on a white background. But there were some elements, where the hyperlink was a purple colour on a grey background and I was surprised to see that this was a fail for link contrast. After some reflection, this has made me realise that although I find something easy to read, this doesn't mean everyone will find it easy to read, and I've recently changed the colour pallette that I want to use, and now I'm concerned about the accessible nature of colours I have picked! I have always had accessibility in mind, but I have been surprised by my findings on the BBC bitesize website. One thing that I liked was the readability tool. It showed that the Bitesize website had a readability for 10 to 11 year olds, which I believe is what their target audience is as it's for younger school aged children to learn and study
